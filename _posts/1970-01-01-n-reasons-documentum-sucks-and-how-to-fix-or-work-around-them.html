--- layout: post title: "n reasons Documentum sucks, and how to fix(or work around) them" ---
  1. The logging is terrible - the sheer amount of logfiles for different services & the different directories that the log files end up in. Logfile rotation, and the pattern (lack of date) in a lot of the log files all add up to make it a big 0/10
  2. There are lots of errors that get thrown by documentum. E.g DM_SESSION_E_RPC_ERR, searching on powerlink should return a result for every single one of them. This one for instance didn't return a result. A quick reference that deals with what to do when you see any given error would be immensely helpful
  3. Patching or upgrading components is not straight forward. Regression is a big problem.
  4. Shear amount of different components (and matching documentation), makes it difficult to find answers to problems and or reference valid information. Powerlink does not help.
  5. Lack of cooperation from EMC Support and Engineers
  6. Resource Hog.
